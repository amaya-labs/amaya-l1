# Amaya L1 — Application and Partner Onboarding

## Overview

Amaya L1 is planned as a permissioned, application-focused network.

Validators, production smart-contract deployment, institutional integrations, and sponsored network access will not be automatically available to anonymous or unverified applicants.

Every applicant must pass the appropriate level of:

- identity verification
- business verification
- technical review
- security review
- legal and regulatory review
- testnet evaluation

Approval to use Amaya Testnet does not guarantee approval for production or mainnet.

## Applicant Categories

### 1. Independent Testnet Developer

Suitable for:

- educational projects
- open-source experiments
- wallet testing
- smart-contract learning
- non-commercial proofs of concept

A registered Philippine business is not necessarily required at this stage.

Minimum requirements:

- full legal name
- verified email address
- country of residence
- GitHub profile or technical portfolio
- project description
- intended testnet use
- requested network access
- confirmation that no real funds or personal data will be used
- agreement to Amaya Testnet rules

Access may be limited to:

- public RPC
- TAMAYA faucet
- test contract deployment
- developer documentation

### 2. Startup or Commercial Project

Suitable for projects intending to:

- serve real users
- operate a marketplace
- integrate payments
- sponsor gas
- deploy production contracts
- access Amaya APIs
- become an approved ecosystem application

The applicant should provide evidence of a legitimate legal entity.

Philippine applicants may submit:

- SEC Certificate of Incorporation or Partnership
- DTI Business Name Certificate for a sole proprietorship
- BIR Certificate of Registration
- current Mayor's or Business Permit
- registered business address
- authorized representative
- official company email
- company website or verified product page
- ownership and management information
- description of business activities

### 3. Institutional Partner

Suitable for:

- transport operators
- parking operators
- cooperatives
- universities
- enterprises
- financial institutions
- government-authorized contractors
- regulated payment providers

Additional requirements may include:

- board resolution or corporate secretary certificate
- authorization naming the official representative
- audited or certified financial information where appropriate
- existing licences and permits
- data-protection policies
- cybersecurity policies
- incident-response plan
- business-continuity plan
- technical integration architecture
- service-level requirements
- designated legal, security, and technical contacts

### 4. Regulated Payment or Financial Project

Applicants proposing:

- fiat-to-crypto services
- crypto-to-fiat services
- payment-system operation
- virtual-asset transfers
- custody
- wallet administration
- remittance
- exchange services
- merchant acquiring
- customer-fund handling

must provide evidence of all licences, registrations, approvals, or regulated partnerships applicable to their activities.

Amaya L1 testnet approval does not authorize an applicant to operate a regulated financial service.

## Business Verification

Amaya may verify submitted information through official sources.

Possible verification checks include:

- SEC company registration
- DTI business-name registration
- BIR registration
- current local business permit
- registered office
- directors, officers, or owner
- beneficial ownership
- authorized representative
- official website and domain
- company email
- public business history
- licence status
- litigation, sanctions, or material adverse information

Documents must be current, readable, and consistent.

Altered, expired, misleading, or unverifiable documents may result in rejection.

## Required Project Information

Every commercial applicant should submit:

### Project Identity

- project name
- company name
- website
- official email
- social-media accounts
- GitHub or source-code repository where appropriate
- names and roles of responsible team members

### Product Description

- problem being solved
- target users
- intended Philippine market
- current development status
- existing users or pilot partners
- expected launch date
- revenue or sustainability model

### Amaya L1 Use Case

- why the project needs blockchain
- why it needs Amaya L1
- data proposed for on-chain storage
- data that will remain off-chain
- expected transaction type
- expected daily and monthly transaction volume
- gas-sponsorship requirements
- required smart contracts
- required RPC or API access
- requested testnet and production permissions

### Technical Architecture

- application architecture diagram
- backend technology
- wallet architecture
- smart-contract design
- relayer design
- data-storage design
- authentication method
- integration endpoints
- third-party dependencies
- upgrade and rollback procedures

### Security Information

- key-management plan
- access-control model
- secret-management process
- smart-contract testing
- vulnerability-handling process
- monitoring and alerting
- backup and recovery
- incident-response contact
- independent audit status where applicable

### Privacy Information

- categories of personal information processed
- purpose of processing
- data-retention period
- encryption controls
- access restrictions
- on-chain and off-chain separation
- privacy contact
- Privacy Impact Assessment where applicable

## Smart-Contract Requirements

Before production approval, contracts may be required to provide:

- source code
- build and deployment instructions
- test coverage
- contract addresses
- compiler and dependency versions
- access-control description
- upgradeability description
- emergency-pause design
- duplicate and replay protection
- external audit report where value or critical records are involved
- ownership and administrator addresses
- deployment and rollback plan

Amaya may reject contracts containing:

- hidden minting authority
- undisclosed administrator powers
- unrestricted token withdrawal
- malicious or deceptive behaviour
- unaudited custody of substantial user funds
- excessive or unnecessary data collection
- dependencies that cannot be independently reviewed

## Application Review Process

```text
Application submitted
        ↓
Identity and business verification
        ↓
Use-case and scope review
        ↓
Technical architecture review
        ↓
Security and privacy review
        ↓
Legal or regulatory review where required
        ↓
Restricted testnet access
        ↓
Testnet performance and behaviour review
        ↓
Controlled pilot approval
        ↓
Production decision
```

Each stage may result in:

```text
APPROVED
CONDITIONALLY APPROVED
MORE INFORMATION REQUIRED
DEFERRED
REJECTED
SUSPENDED
```

## Testnet Approval

A testnet-approved project may receive:

- public or restricted RPC access
- limited TAMAYA faucet access
- test contract-deployment permission
- documentation and technical support
- testnet application identifier
- access to approved integration tools

Testnet projects must not:

- represent TAMAYA as valuable
- collect real customer funds through Amaya Testnet
- store prohibited personal information on-chain
- claim mainnet approval
- claim an Amaya partnership beyond the actual written approval
- use Amaya branding deceptively
- conduct an unauthorized token sale

## Production Approval

Production access requires a separate decision.

Possible requirements include:

- verified legal entity
- signed commercial or ecosystem agreement
- completed security review
- privacy and data-processing agreement
- audited smart contracts
- production support contacts
- approved relayer and deployer wallets
- transaction and gas limits
- monitoring integration
- incident-response obligations
- fee or service agreement
- termination and migration procedure

Production approval may be limited to specific:

- contract addresses
- wallet addresses
- transaction methods
- user groups
- geographic areas
- transaction volumes
- pilot periods

## Foreign Applicants

A foreign company may apply for testnet access.

Before serving Philippine users or conducting regulated or commercial activity in the Philippines, it may be required to provide:

- proof of registration in its home jurisdiction
- directors and beneficial owners
- registered business address
- authorized representative
- Philippine registration or authority where applicable
- local legal or regulated partner where required
- evidence of compliance with applicable Philippine laws

Foreign registration alone does not guarantee production approval.

## Prohibited or High-Risk Applications

Amaya may reject or restrict applications involving:

- fraud or deceptive conduct
- anonymous custody of customer assets
- unlicensed financial services
- unauthorized securities offerings
- money laundering or sanctions evasion
- illegal gambling
- malware or credential theft
- counterfeit documents
- unlawful surveillance
- sale of stolen data
- manipulation of government records
- misleading investment promises
- undisclosed high-risk smart-contract control
- activity that may damage users or the Amaya network

## Continuing Obligations

Approved projects must:

- keep registration and licence information current
- report ownership or management changes
- report material security incidents
- report smart-contract upgrades
- maintain official technical contacts
- comply with agreed transaction limits
- protect user data
- cooperate with incident investigation
- stop affected services when instructed during a critical incident
- avoid misleading use of Amaya branding

## Suspension or Removal

Access may be suspended or removed for:

- false application information
- expired business registration
- loss of required licence
- security compromise
- malicious transactions
- excessive RPC abuse
- prohibited data written on-chain
- unauthorized contract deployment
- deceptive marketing
- failure to address critical vulnerabilities
- breach of an Amaya agreement

Removal from Amaya L1 does not erase already confirmed blockchain history.

## Public Approved-Application Registry

Amaya may later publish a registry showing:

- approved application name
- verified company
- application status
- approved contract addresses
- official website
- network environment
- approval date
- security-review status
- suspension or revocation status

This helps users distinguish legitimate applications from fake copies.

## Current Status

The application and partner-onboarding framework is currently a proposed public policy.

Amaya L1 is not currently accepting production deployments or institutional applications.
